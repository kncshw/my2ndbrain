# Historical Analog — 2021 Peak / 2022 Bear

The cleanest available parallel to today's setup: semi-shortage-driven rally (COVID-era) peaking late 2021, followed by a Fed-driven bear market through 2022. Used as a base-rate reference for sizing risk and watching for the same setup patterns to repeat.

## RSI at the actual peak — the most important finding

| Index | Wkly RSI Nov 19, 2021 (first top) | Wkly RSI Dec 27, 2021 (second top) | Today (May 8, 2026) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SMH** | **74.2** | 64.9 ← *divergence* | **84.5** |
| SOX | 73.6 | 65.8 | 85.5 |
| QQQ | 73.1 | (~63) | 74.2 |
| NDX | 73.1 | (~63) | 74.2 |

**Three observations to remember:**

### 1. The 2021 top happened at SMH weekly RSI 74.2 — not at an extreme
The first top printed at the 95th percentile of all-time. Not the 99th. **Tops don't require extreme RSI.** What killed the rally was Fed/macro, not RSI exhaustion.

### 2. The textbook bearish divergence was the actual sell signal
Between Nov 19 and Dec 27, SMH made a marginally higher closing high ($156.10 → $158.28) while RSI dropped from 74.2 to 64.9. **Higher price + lower RSI = bearish divergence.** This was THE technical signal. It took ~5 weeks to form.

### 3. Today is MORE stretched than the 2021 first top (by ~10 RSI points)
- 2021 SMH wkly RSI at peak: 74.2 (95th percentile)
- Today SMH wkly RSI: 84.5 (99.9th percentile)
- Net: today's *technical* picture is more extreme; today's *catalyst* picture is less clear

## SMH–NDX RSI spread

| Date | SMH wkRSI | NDX wkRSI | Spread |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2021-11-19 (peak) | 74.2 | 73.1 | **+1.1** (in sync) |
| 2026-05-08 (today) | 84.5 | 74.2 | **+10.3** (wide) |

In 2021, semis and NDX peaked together — broad participation in the top. **Today's wide spread means semis are leading hard with NDX behind.** The "everyone joins the top together" pattern from 2021 hasn't formed yet. Either NDX catches up (spread compresses, full euphoria) or semis come down to NDX (semis correct in isolation).

**Shape-of-risk read:**
- 2021: "we know a bear is coming, just don't know exactly when" — known catalyst, telegraphed
- Today: "the powder keg looks bigger but we don't know what the spark is" — bigger technical setup, no clear trigger

## Implications for hedging today

If a similar bear plays out from May 2026 SMH = $566:
- 45% drawdown → SMH at ~$311
- 50% drawdown → SMH at ~$283
- Horizon: 9–13 months

**Calibration for the hedge:**
- A 6-month SMH put at $510 (10% OTM) would pay massively if SMH retraces toward $300s
- Cost (~3–4% of notional) is small vs. -45% unhedged loss
- Path is a grind, not a crash → short-dated puts could expire worthless even if bear is starting → 6-month tenor is appropriate
- Rolling the hedge through the decline is feasible (sell as puts pay, buy lower strikes)

## What to watch for — patterns to detect that "2022 is starting again"

In priority order:

1. **Bearish divergence on SMH or SOX weekly RSI.** Price prints marginal new high, RSI fails to confirm with new high. **THE pattern.** Took 5 weeks in 2021 (Nov 19 → Dec 27).

2. **SMH–NDX RSI spread compresses.** Today's +10.3 narrows. If NDX rallies while semis stay flat (spread compressing), that's NDX "joining the top" — same pattern as 2021.

3. **First 15% drop happens fast, no obvious catalyst.** In 2021–22, SMH dropped 15.7% in 3 months BEFORE the Fed acted. If you see SMH down 15% from here without a clear macro event, that's the trend-exhaustion signal.

4. **Step-down pattern with each macro event.** -15% → -22% → -31% → -45% over the 9 months. If hedge is in place, each step is a chance to roll strikes lower.
