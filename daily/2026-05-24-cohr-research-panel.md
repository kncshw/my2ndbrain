# Analyst 1 — COHR Product-to-AI Mapping

**Stock context:** $377.57, mkt cap ~$73.9B, +381% 1Y, +102% YTD. Fwd P/E ~47. **This is priced as a pure AI-optics name, not a hybrid.**

## Segment Breakdown — Q3 FY26 ($1.81B total, +21% YoY / +27% pro-forma)

COHR collapsed reporting in FY26 from three segments to **TWO**: Datacenter & Communications + Industrial. Old-segment visibility is gone.

| Reporting line | Q3 FY26 Rev | YoY | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Datacenter & Communications** | **$1,361.6M** | **+40.5%** | 75% of total. Datacenter sub-line +13% QoQ / +37% YoY; Comms +16% QoQ / +60% YoY |
| **Industrial** | **$444.0M** | **−16.1%** | Ex-Lasers + residual Materials. Cell-welding, marking, semi processes |

- GAAP GM 37.7% (+243 bps); non-GAAP GM 39.6% (+105 bps)
- Non-GAAP EPS $1.41 (+55% YoY)
- Cash $1.59B; LT debt $3.18B; SiC sale proceeds $437.3M YTD
- **Capex $290M in Q (YTD $547M)** — explicit ramp for InP / transceiver / CPO capacity
- Q4 FY26 guide: **$1.91–2.05B rev**, 39–41% non-GAAP GM, $1.52–1.72 EPS

**Reality check:** Datacenter +40% YoY is the entire story. Industrial dropped 16% YoY — the new 2-segment structure conveniently makes the cyclical drag invisible at op level.

## CPO Position — the bet-the-company story

**March 2, 2026 NVIDIA partnership:**
- **$2B NVIDIA equity investment** in COHR
- **Multi-year, multi-billion-$ purchase commitment** "extending through the end of the decade" (Anderson)
- **Nonexclusive** — NVDA can still source from LITE, but COHR gets capacity/access rights to 5 additional product families
- Anderson on CPO: *"CPO expands our role in AI data center architectures, particularly in the scale-up portion of the network, where optics is expected to increasingly complement and over time displace copper."*
- Timing: **CPO scale-out revenue H2 calendar 2026; scale-up CPO revenue H2 2027.**
- TAM framing: "**>$15B**" addressable

## Bull-case quantification (back-of-envelope)

- **800G transceiver ASP** ~$800-1,200; GM 35-45% as yields mature
- **1.6T transceiver ASP** ~$1,500-2,500 at launch
- **Per AI rack (NVL72):** ~72 GPUs × ~9 optical links = ~650 transceivers/rack. At ~$1000 ASP = **~$650K transceiver TAM per rack**. COHR ~10-15% share = **$65-100K COHR content per AI rack**
- **Order book signal:** "**step function increase in order book, backlog at record level**" — customer visibility now extends to **calendar 2028**

## Honest Reality Check

Decomposition of Q3 +21% YoY:
- **Genuine AI**: 800G/1.6T transceivers, EML chips on 6-in InP, CPO families with NVIDIA, CW lasers for SiPh = **~60%+ of revenue, growing 35-40% YoY**
- **AI-adjacent**: 400ZR/800ZR coherent — hyperscaler DC interconnect + legacy telecom — ~15% of rev
- **Not AI**: Industrial $444M (fiber lasers for welding, EV battery, excimer for displays/semi). **Down 16% YoY.** Calling this "AI semi-supercycle" is a stretch
- True AI-driven revenue is roughly **$1.0-1.2B/quarter** (DC + slice of comms), not the full $1.81B top line

**Anderson verbatim on AI dominance:**
> "Customer demand remains exceptionally strong with no signs of attenuation. Our visibility continues to extend further into the future, with orders now reaching into calendar 2028."
> "We are at the center of an extraordinary expansion in optical networking infrastructure."

Notably absent: any meaningful commentary on EV battery, general industrial recovery, semi lithography. **The new CEO has steered the narrative entirely to AI optics. Industrial is being de-emphasized publicly even though it's still 25% of revenue.**

# Analyst 2 — COHR vs LITE vs FN vs IPGP

## Side-by-side positioning

|---|---|---|---|---|

| Core franchise | Hybrid: AI optics + industrial + materials | DC optics pure-play + 3D sensing | Optical EMS contract mfr | Industrial fiber lasers pure-play |
| AI touchpoint | 800G/1.6T + EML + CPO laser source + InP wafers | 800G/1.6T + **200G/lane EMLs (sole-source)** + CPO laser source | Assembles for COHR/LITE/Ciena/NVDA | None meaningful |
| Defensibility | **Vertical: owns 6" InP fab → chip → module → laser source** | Vertical at chip: only volume 200G/lane EMLs | Scale + sticky customer footprints | Vertical fiber-laser; IP moat eroding (TRUMPF) |

**DC optics (COHR vs LITE):** Both shipping 800G; both ramping 1.6T. Differentiator: **200G-per-lane EMLs** — LITE is the only volume supplier today, making it the upstream chokepoint COHR depends on AND competes with. COHR's edge is **scope** (sells everything from wafer to module). LITE's edge is **purity** of DC-optics narrative.

**CPO laser source:** Mar 2, 2026 NVIDIA committed **$4B split evenly — $2B COHR + $2B LITE**. Multi-year purchase commitments. Quantum-X is GA; Spectrum-X targets 2H26. **Co-winners**, with LITE arguably better positioned on ultra-high-power CPO lasers.

**EMS (FN):** ~30% of FN revenue is NVIDIA; optical comms 73% of revenue. **FN is the supply-chain physical layer** — when COHR/LITE/Marvell sell a transceiver, FN often assembled it.

**Industrial lasers (COHR vs IPGP):** IPGP Q1 2026 Industrial Solutions +21% YoY to $227.6M (86% of rev) — recovering but adj GM compressed to 37.8% on tariffs + TRUMPF settlement. COHR Industrial $444M Q3 FY26 — roughly 2x IPGP at higher scale. **COHR wins on scale and isn't bleeding on litigation.**

## Verdict — Ranking

| Dimension | Winner | Loser |
|---|---|---|
| **AI tailwind capture** | **LITE** (highest concentration, sole-source EMLs) | IPGP (none) |
| **Defensibility (vertical integration)** | **COHR** (full stack: wafer → chip → module → laser source) | FN (assembler, replaceable in theory) |
| **Valuation entry point** | **COHR** at 11x P/S vs LITE 30x P/S | LITE — priciest by a mile |
| **CPO positioning** | **LITE** edges (200G/lane EML, largest CPO order in history) | Neither dominant — Cisco SiPho + foundries split the rest |

**Overall: LITE wins on AI exposure, COHR wins on risk-adjusted setup.**

- **Highest AI-pure upside:** LITE. Cleanest story, technological chokepoint. Priced for it (30x P/S).
- **Best risk-adjusted AI optics play right now: COHR.** Same NVDA $2B commitment as LITE, same 1.6T ramp, vertical integration LITE doesn't have, plus industrial + materials as ballast — **at less than half LITE's revenue multiple.**
- **Best "shovels" play with hidden leverage:** FN. But NVDA concentration ~30% is a real tail risk.
- **Don't own for AI:** IPGP. Industrial cycle name with litigation/tariff drag. The +66% YTD is borrowed beta.

**Pick: COHR.** Same CPO endorsement as LITE, broader moat, trading at 11x sales vs LITE's 30x. LITE is the better business *today*; COHR is the better stock from here.

# Analyst 3 — COHR Moat vs Chinese Optics

## Chinese Optical Transceiver Landscape

| Company | Position |
|---|---|
| **Innolight (300308.SZ)** | Global #1 in 800G shipments, ~35% global / **>50% of NVIDIA 800G procurement**. First to complete 1.6T qual with NVDA, projected **50-60% share of 1.6T** in 2025 |
| **Eoptolink (300502.SZ)** | Co-leader; together with Innolight control **~60% of NVIDIA 800G volume**. 1.6T LRO/MCF/OSFP at OFC 2025 |
| **Accelink (002281.SZ)** | State-backed (FiberHome sub), telecom/commodity, slower at bleeding edge |
| **Hisense Broadband** | High-volume FTTH + datacom; cost leader |
| **Source Photonics** | Chinese-owned US-based; mid-tier |

**Bottom line: Chinese transceiver assemblers ALREADY WON — they ARE the primary NVIDIA suppliers, not aspirants.**

## Chinese Industrial Laser Landscape

- **Han's Laser (002008.SZ)** — FY2025 ~$2.67B (+27% YoY), NPAT +82%; benefiting from AI server/HDI PCB capex
- **Raycus + Maxphotonics** — dominate Chinese fiber lasers; Raycus pricing **~35% below IPG** in 6kW+, ~50% below in pulsed
- Top 4 (IPG + Raycus + Maxphotonics + nLight) = ~80% of China fiber laser market
- IPG China revenue collapsed; HP CW revenue down ~7% as Asians slashed prices

## Technology Gap

| Layer | COHR moat? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **EML / InP laser chips** | **Real, durable** | EMLs inside Chinese modules come from COHR/Lumentum/Broadcom/Marvell. Only Lumentum ships 200G/lane EMLs at volume today. COHR brought up world's first 6-inch InP line Aug 2025 — capacity moat |
| **Transceiver assembly** | **Eroding fast** | Innolight/Eoptolink have scale, yield, NVDA sockets. COHR is share-loser in unit terms at 800G |
| **CPO** | **TBD, likely defensible near-term** | First sockets at NVDA/Broadcom go to US-aligned suppliers; export-control optics on AI gear plausible by 2027 |
| **VCSELs (high-perf)** | Solid (with Lumentum) | Chinese (Vertilite) only meaningful at LiDAR/consumer |
| **High-power fiber lasers (>6kW)** | **Largely lost in China** | Raycus/Maxphotonics at parity, 30-50% cheaper |

## AI Datacenter Switching Risk

Hyperscalers and NVIDIA **already buy Chinese optics today** via ODM — Innolight is NOT on Entity List. Risk is NOT a binary ban; it is gradual ASP/margin compression as Innolight wins 1.6T sockets COHR thought it had locked. **Geopolitical optionality cuts both ways:** any tightening of optics into Entity List would re-route share to COHR/LITE overnight — fat-tail upside.

## COHR China Revenue Exposure

- **FY2025: ~$680M / $5.81B ≈ 11.7%** (vs 13.2% FY24, 11.2% FY23)
- Mix skew: industrial Lasers more China-exposed (grew only +3% FY25 vs Networking +49%)
- China share has compressed from mid-teens to ~12% as AI/networking outpaces

## Verdict (1-10 moat ratings)

| Capability | Score | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) High-end EML/InP chips** | **8/10** | Load-bearing wall of the COHR thesis. Capacity-backed, multi-year. Chinese in-house EML still sub-100G and unproven |
| **(b) 800G/1.6T transceiver assembly** | **4/10** | Innolight already won 800G; 1.6T share split is COHR's to lose. Margins, not existence, are the question |
| **(c) Industrial fiber laser in China** | **2/10** | Lost. Same playbook that gutted IPGP |

**Net:** COHR's durable moat is **upstream (chips/wafers), not downstream (modules)**. If you own COHR, you're really betting on **EML/InP scarcity and 6-inch InP ramp** — NOT on beating Innolight at module assembly. Industrial lasers are a melting ice cube in China.
