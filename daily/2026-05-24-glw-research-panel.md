# Analyst 3 — GLW Moat vs Chinese Fiber & Glass

## Chinese Optical Fiber Landscape

"Big Four" Chinese fiber makers — **YOFC (06869.HK)**, **Hengtong (600487.SH)**, **FiberHome**, **ZTT (600522.SH)** — dominate China's domestic market and aggressively pushing globally. At MWC 2026 all four showcased hollow-core fiber and "anti-resonant" designs; **Hengtong claims a 2025/26 anti-resonant breakthrough**. YOFC's "AI-2030" strategy (mid-2025) targets hyperscale DC + hollow-core. YOFC has 1.2B fiber-km deployed across 100+ countries — **NOT a niche player**.

## Chinese Display Glass Landscape

- **BOE (000725.SZ)** is the panel king but is a glass *consumer*, not substrate maker — depends on Corning's Wuhan Gen 10.5 plant + AGC/NEG
- **Tunghsu (000413.SZ)** has been financially distressed since 2019; production continues but no Gen 10.5 R&D funding
- **Triumph (600552.SH)** remains Gen 8.5 / non-leading-edge
- **Two adverse 2025 developments for BOE:** (i) ITC banned BOE OLED imports into US for ~14 years after losing Samsung suit (Aug 2025); (ii) draft NDAA proposes adding BOE to DoD "Chinese military companies" list. **Both REDUCE BOE's ability to displace incumbents in Western supply chains.**

## Technology Gap

| Product | GLW position | Chinese threat |
|---|---|---|
| Hollow-core / low-loss DC fiber | Microsoft exclusive partner; NVDA long-term deal; 3 new US plants | YOFC/Hengtong closing fast; field trials with China telcos only |
| Pre-terminated AI DC assemblies | Dominant — 10x US connectivity expansion | Real tolerance gap |
| Gen 10.5 LCD substrate | GLW + AGC + NEG oligopoly intact | Tunghsu broken; BOE buys from GLW |
| OLED-capable substrate | Strong | Weak |
| Gorilla Glass / cover | 48% global share; AGC Dragontrail 31%; Schott #3 | Lens Tech is a *finisher*, not glass maker |
| Specialty (Valor vials, semi) | GLW + Schott + NEG | Negligible |

## AI DC Switching Risk

**Low for US hyperscalers, high for Chinese hyperscalers.** MSFT, META ($6B deal), GOOG, AWS, NVIDIA are locked into GLW for AI DC fiber.

**The REVERSE problem matters more:** **China imposed 37.9% anti-dumping duty on Corning G.654.C fiber effective Sept 2025, valid through April 2028** (MOFCOM ruling) — China's first-ever anti-circumvention finding. **Functionally locks GLW OUT of Chinese AI DC builds**, ceding that TAM to YOFC/Hengtong/ZTT. Alibaba, ByteDance, Tencent will not buy Corning fiber at 38% premium.

## GLW China Revenue Exposure

**CORRECTION (added 2026-05-24 after user pushback):** GLW does NOT separately disclose Mainland China revenue. The 10-K aggregates to **Asia Pacific = $7.65B = 47.3% of FY2025 revenue** (with North America $7.11B / 44.0% and Europe $1.41B / 8.7%).

Mainland China is one component of that Asia Pacific bucket. Plausible decomposition:
- Mainland China: ~$3.0-4.0B → **~20-22% of total** (best estimate, not company-disclosed)
- South Korea (Samsung Display historic JV): ~$1.5-2.0B
- Taiwan (Taichung specialty/display): ~$1.0-1.5B
- Japan + India + SE Asia: balance

**The original "25-30% China" claim was at the high end of triangulation, not directly disclosed. Honest revised estimate: ~20-22%.**

Display still the largest China-exposed segment; flows through Chinese panel makers (BOE, CSOT, HKC). Chinese panel demand weak 2023-2025; Corning offset with double-digit 2024 price hikes + yen hedging — Display still printed ~25%+ margins.

## Verdict (1-10 moat scores)

| Capability | Score | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) AI DC optical fiber** | **8/10** | Hyperscale lock-in (MSFT, META, NVIDIA + 2 unnamed), hollow-core lead, US mfg pivot. Erosion only in China (MOFCOM duties) |
| **(b) Display glass substrate** | **7/10** | Has NOT lost to BOE — BOE buys from Corning. Margins still 25%+; moat intact but not growing |
| **(c) Gorilla Glass / cover** | **7/10** | 48% share, Apple anchor, expanding to auto. Schott #2. No Chinese glass-maker threat |
| **(d) Specialty (Valor, semi, optics)** | **8/10** | Oligopoly with Schott/NEG; Chinese minimal; benefits from biosecurity/pharma reshoring |

**Bottom line:** GLW has NOT lost the display war to BOE — BOE is a customer. The real, under-appreciated risk is the **Sept 2025 Chinese 37.9% anti-dumping duty** that caps GLW's China fiber TAM and accelerates YOFC/Hengtong global mid-tier displacement. AI moat with US/Western hyperscalers is genuinely deep through at least 2028.
